What is POPI?

POPI, short for the Protection of Personal Information Act, is a South African data privacy law that regulates the collection, processing, and protection of personal information. The primary purpose of POPI is to safeguard individuals' personal data from unauthorized access and use.

Our Commitment to POPI Compliance

At MWRK, we are committed to ensuring strict adherence to POPI regulations. We take the protection of personal information seriously and have implemented robust policies and procedures to maintain compliance with the law.

Key POPI Principles

POPI is founded on several core principles that guide how personal information should be handled:

  • Accountability: We take responsibility for the personal information we collect and process.
  • Processing Limitation: We only collect and process personal information for lawful and specific purposes.
  • Consent: We obtain consent from individuals before collecting their personal data.
  • Information Quality: We ensure the accuracy and integrity of personal information we hold.
  • Security Safeguards: We have robust security measures in place to protect personal data from unauthorized access or breaches.
  • Data Subject Rights: We respect individuals' rights regarding their personal information.

How We Protect Personal Information

Protecting personal information is a top priority for us. We have implemented the following measures:

  • Data Encryption: We use encryption technologies to secure personal data during transmission and storage.
  • Access Control: We restrict access to personal information to authorized personnel only.
  • Data Retention: We retain personal information only for the period required by law and for the purpose for which it was collected.
  • Data Breach Response: We have a response plan in place to address and notify affected parties in the event of a data breach.
